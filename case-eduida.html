The platform by and for language students

Eduida www.eduida.es is a platform to practise English for free and in an appealing way. The idea came while I was studying languages from experiencing firsthand the difficulty of finding any free tool to practise that fit the current UX quality standard. Besides, not only is it a gift for the community but also a good laboratory for us to test and put in practice the new knowledge we acquire from investigation.

This project was carried out in collaboration with Álvaro Belmonte who is in charge of development. However, I, as a product owner and designer, have run all strategic planning, content creation and provision of end-to-end UX solutions. Listening & Research

By the time I studied French in 2020, I found it difficult to find free language-learning tools with a good user experience. I often found myself frustrated with clunky interfaces from the early 2000s, poorly designed lessons and even useless exercices. Talking and deepening with my classmates I realized we shared the same frustrations. However I also noticed some difference that made me aware of the different needs the target audience have. Hence, I decided to design my own language-learning tool with all the information I gathered from the conversation with my peers.

Think

Once I knew what people needed and how the market was divided, it was high time to kick off the challenge. I had to come up with a recognisable brand and product which were also helpful for our target audience. To get to that point, I highlighted the main points of both brand and product manifesto:

We need to be easy to access

We need to be easy to use

We need to be affordable

We need to be appealing to use

We need to be sincere

We need to be close and friendly

Putting this manifesto to work turned into a new brand concept: Eduida which shortens from Educación Divertida (Fun Education in Spanish) and also is easy to remember since phonetically sounds alike.

For the imagotype or symbol I mix different concepts. the E from eduida, a shelf of books and a puzzle, since it pretends to be a place where books are like games and content is gamified.

Design System

As part of the Lean UX methodology, it was important for us having a brief design system that could be easily and quickly replicated to set the visual aspect and parameters of all the elements which would impact on product, branding and marketing. For that reason, we created a first stage design system to kick off.

Foundations:

Content:
Familiarity with Eduida is essential to creating experiences that are consistent, that provide an interoperability of experience with other offerings, and that represent Eduida’s personality which comes from the manifesto. That’s a clear voice which speaks directly what wants to be said, in a short and understandable way. No second intentions are meant. No harmful messages are displayed. Eduida talks loud, clear and kindless.

Color:
There was not a simple colour scheme set for this project. Any colour from this gradient spectrum would be appropriate and suitable. However, the main colours to be used will be the following ones:

Spacing:
Like in most of the famous design systems, space was set to grow over a 4px base. Any multiple of 4 would be ok to be used but ideally the ratio set was (4, 8, 16, 24, 32, 48, 60, 72) By that we assured elements to ensemble together like LEGO bricks.

Typography:
Since this project started as a free educational platform, it was important for us cutting as many costs as possible whilst using good performance elements. As a result, we decided that the chosen typographies would be these free well-matching ones.

Iconography:
Icons can be found all along the product and on social media posts as well. Because of that there is a need for a broad icon spectrum that allows icons to be in positive and negative. In addition icons should be stroke icons but also filled icons to ensure the best of the uses.

Information Architecture & Nav Map

From the conversations with my peers I knew how the basic structure had to be. I also knew that it would not be possible having only one flow as there are different types of language students with different needs and knowledge. So let’s see the hot topics I knew I had to cover:

  • A hub for selecting what to practice: verbs, vocabulary, grammar...
  • A hub for selecting how to practice. If with one type of exercise or another
  • A schoolish section to learn the theory of the exercises
  • An appealing landing page to lead people to act
  • An easy access to and from social media so that people were easly engaged
  • A free no register trial so that people could have a look to how this is before registering.

How we improved Eduida by using Hotjar

By using Hotjar, we had real data on user usage and have been able to locate and correct important pain points affecing usabilty. As an example, I show next how we solved 3 pain points just inside the sign up and log in process.

  • As per reccordings not seeing password was a problem for some users
  • As per reccordings account creation message was not obvious and people struggled trying to sign up several times
  • Thanks to reccordings I saw that the link of the log in button must have been detached at some point

All these issues meant a hole in our system. I wonder though how it is possible only one person wrote us saying he could not log in after registration.
